Research

Hello there! My name is Dongyan (pronounced Dong-yan), I am a Master's student at Integrated Program in Neuroscience at McGill University and Mila, supervised by Dr. Blake Richards. My research interest lies on the intersection of artificial intelligence and neuroscience, with a focus on the role of memory in reinforcement learning.

I graduated from University of Toronto with Hon. B. Sc. in 2019, majoring in physiology while double-minoring in mathematics and psychology. During my undergraduate studies, I received a combination of data analysis and experimental neuroscience training. As a University Research Opportunity Program (UROP) awardee, I investigated in vitro calcium regulation of gamma oscillation in mouse hippocampus at Prescott Lab at Sickkids Hospital. My work at RIKEN on human genome annotation under the supervision of Dr. Michiel de Hoon received the best poster award at Summer Undergraduate Research Symposium (click here to see the poster). I also had the pleasure to work as the lab manager at Duncan Lab for a year.

I aspire to dedicate my graduate training to addressing questions such as how we can mathematically model brain activities and how biological neural circuits can inspire artificial intelligence, as well as to developing machine learning tools that can be applied to neuroscience data.
